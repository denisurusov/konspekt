## Human — exclude per-turn and end-of-session; keep manual + LLM venture
Exclude "per turn". Keep "on human manual command". Instruct the LLM to venture
guesses when an atom is ready for syncing. Do not rely on "end of session"
because it isn't very clear what a session is, let alone jt being a standard
concept across LLM vendors

## Assistant — two event-triggers, mapped onto propose vs persist
"you've thrown out cadence (clock/turn rhythm) entirely in favor of event
triggers, which is the more robust move precisely because it doesn't lean on
turn or session semantics that vary by vendor. Two events fire the loop:

- Atom-ready (LLM-judged) → the maintainer watches the thread and, when it
  believes a durable atom has crystallized — a decision landed, a concept got
  named, a task resolved — it ventures it as a proposal. Low-stakes by
  construction: everything it ventures is review: proposed, never accepted on
  its own. This is the propose/sync side.
- Human manual command (sync / persist / sync_persist, plus the acceptance
  verbs) → the deterministic, authoritative side. This is what accepts and what
  writes durably. The persist side."

"One caveat worth baking in: 'venture when an atom is ready' needs a readiness
bar, or it quietly degrades back into the per-turn noise you just excluded. The
instruction has to bias toward holding until something has actually settled, not
narrating every exchange."

## Human — readiness is LLM-judgable given the node types, but imperfect; ratify
LLMs should really be able to understand - given our types of nodes - when an
atom is ready, but I agree it isn't going to be a perfect mechanism; go ahead
and update Operating.md.
